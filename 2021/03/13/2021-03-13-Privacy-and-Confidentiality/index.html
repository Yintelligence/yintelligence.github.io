Privacy and Confidentiality

Introduction

One of the criteria for Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval is that, when appropriate, the research reflects adequate provisions to protect the privacy of subjects and maintain the confidentiality of data (Protection of Human Subjects 2017). This module clarifies the difference between privacy and confidentiality.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Describe what is meant by the term "privacy."
  • Identify ways to protect subject privacy.
  • Describe what is meant by the term "confidentiality."
  • Identify ways to protect subject confidentiality.

Privacy

Privacy refers to the right to control access to ourselves and our personal information. It means that we have the right to control the degree, timing, and conditions for sharing our bodies, thoughts, and experiences with others.

Privacy must be protected before and during the recruitment of subjects, the consent process, and participation in the research activity.

Methods to protect subject privacy include:

  • Conducting research activities in a private setting
  • Ensuring that private data are not collected without the individual's knowledge and consent (observation of public behaviour is okay)

Confidentiality

Confidentiality refers to agreements made between researchers and subjects, through the consent process, about if and how researchers will protect information provided by the subjects.

These agreements may include:

  • Plans for retention of identifiers associated with the data
  • Methods for safeguarding data (such as, encryption or limited access to identifiable data).

The protection of confidentiality occurs after the data is collected and in the researcher’s possession.

Summary

Federal regulations require research to include adequate provisions to protect the privacy of subjects and maintain the confidentiality of data (Protection of Human Subjects 2017).

Subjects should be informed during the consent process of the methods that will be used to maintain the confidentiality of their identifiable information and the possible risks of disclosure of this information outside of the research study.

Reference

  • Protection of Human Subjects, 45 CFR § 46 (2017).

Quiz

Question 1

Identify the term that refers to agreements made between researchers and subjects, through the consent process, about if and how researchers will protect information provided by the subjects.

  • Confidentiality
  • Privacy
  • Ownership
  • Identifiable

Comment:

Confidentiality refers to agreements made between researchers and subjects, through the consent process, about if and how researchers will protect information provided by the subjects.

Question 2

Which of the following is a measure researchers can use to protect the confidentiality of subject data?

  • Consent subjects in private rooms where no one can overhear.
  • Approach potential subjects in a private way.
  • Disclose that interview questions will include sensitive topics (such as, income, health status, and sexual behaviour).
  • Keep sensitive and identifiable data in encrypted files on a password protected hard drive.

Comment:

The correct answer is “Keep data on sensitive issues in encrypted files in a password protected hard drive.” This is the only answer that addresses maintaining confidentiality of research data. The other answers all address protecting subject privacy.

Question 3

Identify the term that refers to the right to control access to ourselves and to our personal information.

  • Privacy
  • Ownership
  • Identifiable
  • Confidentiality

Comment:

Privacy refers to the right to control access to ourselves and to our personal information.

Question 4

A method to protect subject’s privacy in research would be:

  • Closing the study if there are not enough subjects to provide meaningful data
  • Conducting research activities in a private setting
  • Requiring parental consent and minor assent for a minor subject to participate in the research
  • Including a staged-consent process (asking for consent at the beginning of every study activity)

Comment:

Methods to protect subject privacy include conducting research activities in a private setting or ensuring that data are not collected without the individual's knowledge and consent.
